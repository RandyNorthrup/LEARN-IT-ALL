## Recommendations (Priority Order)

### 1. Add Practice/Review Questions to All Chapter 6 Lessons (HIGH)
All 8 lessons (053–060) need 7–10 practice questions with answers. This is the single largest structural gap. Suggested format: match Ch5's "Practice Exercises" style with answers provided.

### 2. Add References to Chapters 4 and 6 (HIGH)
- **Chapter 4** (12 lessons): Add References citing NIST SP 800 series, CIS Benchmarks, vendor documentation, relevant RFCs.
- **Chapter 6** (7 lessons, 053–059): Add References citing IEEE 802.11-2020, Wi-Fi Alliance specifications, Bluetooth SIG Core Spec, NFC Forum, CWNA Study Guide, relevant RFCs.

### 3. Standardize Section Naming Across All Chapters (MEDIUM)
Choose and apply consistently:
- `## Key Takeaways` OR `## Summary` (not both)
- `## Review Questions` OR `## Practice Exercises` (not both)  
- `## References` OR `## Additional Resources` (not both)

### 4. Address Content Overlap (HIGH)
- **Lesson 038 ↔ 056**: Refactor lesson 038 to be a security-architecture overview that cross-references lesson 056 for wireless protocol details. Alternatively, restructure lesson 056's content to focus on protocol evolution and implementation while lesson 038 focuses on attack/defense patterns.
- **Lesson 033 ↔ 058**: Add cross-references. Lesson 033 should provide a general AAA framework overview; lesson 058 should focus on wireless-specific implementation.

### 5. Fix Lesson 060 Structural Issues (MEDIUM)
- Change H1 to `# Lesson 60: Wireless Troubleshooting`
- Add `## Introduction` heading before the opening paragraph
- Restructure `## Summary > ### Key Takeaways` to match Ch6 pattern: `## Key Takeaways`

### 6. Standardize Frontmatter Fields (MEDIUM)
Either add `sidebar_label` and `description` to all lessons, or remove them from lessons 046–052. Recommendation: add them to all lessons since they provide useful metadata.

### 7. Consider Expanding Shorter Lessons (LOW)
Lessons 054 (608 lines), 055 (642 lines), and 043 (625 lines) may benefit from additional content to reach the 3000-word target. Possible additions: more worked examples, troubleshooting scenarios, or additional practice problems.
