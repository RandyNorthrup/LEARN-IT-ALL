# Step 6

Remember that a `class` attribute can have multiple values. To separate your white keys from your black keys, you'll add a second `class` value of `black--key`. Add this to your second, third, fifth, sixth, and seventh `.key` elements.

## Starter Code

```html
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8" />
    <title>Piano</title>
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0" />
  </head>
  <body>
    --fcc-editable-region--
    <div id="piano">
      <div class="keys">
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
        <div class="key"></div>
      </div>
    </div>
    --fcc-editable-region--
  </body>
</html>
```

## Hints

1. Your second `.key` element should also have a `class` of `black--key`.
2. Your third `.key` should have `black--key` in the `class`.
3. Your fifth `.key` should have `black--key` in the `class`.
4. Your sixth `.key` should have `black--key` in the `class`.
5. Your seventh `.key` should have `black--key` in the `class`.
6. You should have five `.black--key` elements.
7. You should have seven `.key` elements.
